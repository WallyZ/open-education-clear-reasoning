# CR-101 Clear Reasoning Foundations

## Course Role

CR-101 is the suite-facing adapter for the existing Clear Reasoning Foundations curriculum. It teaches disciplined attention, definitions, propositions, proof craft, inference, dialectic, fallacy repair, rhetoric, disagreement, and transfer.

## Duration

12 to 16 weeks depending on learner placement. Each module includes source orientation, lecture, deliberate practice, written analysis, spoken explanation, dialogue or objection practice, and revision. Full lecture media should target 45 to 55 minutes; short fixtures may be shorter.

## Prerequisites

Learners need basic literacy, willingness to revise, and willingness to represent opposing views fairly. The course adapts examples and pacing while preserving the standard for disciplined reasoning.

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of the course, the learner can:

- `clear-reasoning:objectives/course/cr-101/attention-and-distinction`: Slow down, identify the kind of question being asked, and separate related claims.
- `clear-reasoning:objectives/course/cr-101/definitions-and-categories`: Define terms, test borderline cases, and repair category mistakes.
- `clear-reasoning:objectives/course/cr-101/propositions-and-contradiction`: Extract propositions and identify contradiction, implication, and qualification.
- `clear-reasoning:objectives/course/cr-101/proof-craft`: Reconstruct proof-like reasoning from definitions, premises, and ordered steps.
- `clear-reasoning:objectives/course/cr-101/inference-and-validity`: Distinguish valid form, strong support, weak support, and persuasive appearance.
- `clear-reasoning:objectives/course/cr-101/dialectical-questions`: Ask ordered questions that reveal structure without merely attacking.
- `clear-reasoning:objectives/course/cr-101/fallacy-repair`: Detect fallacies and manipulative moves, then repair the argument into a fairer form.
- `clear-reasoning:objectives/course/cr-101/ethical-rhetoric`: Persuade without hiding weak reasoning or manipulating the audience.
- `clear-reasoning:objectives/course/cr-101/disagreement-discipline`: Steelman and answer an opposing view with discipline and charity.
- `clear-reasoning:objectives/course/cr-101/capstone-defense`: Defend and revise a clear reasoning portfolio in writing, speech, and dialogue.

## Full Lecture And Practice Session Blueprint

Each week should produce a 45 to 55 minute lecture or seminar, a 60 to 90 minute practice session, and one revised artifact. The lecture teaches the reasoning move, the practice session forces performance under pressure, and the revision proves that correction changed the learner's habits.

| Week | Full Lecture Arc | Practice Session | Performance Pressure |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Attention, question type, term/claim/evidence separation, and why speed hides confusion. | Distinction ladder on a live disputed phrase; listener repeats the repaired distinction. | Friendly listener asks for one concrete example and one non-example. |
| 2 | Genus, difference, examples, borderline cases, exclusions, and category mistakes. | Term Lock plus definition tree for a civic, moral, or workplace term. | Sincere skeptic tests the definition with hard borderline cases. |
| 3 | Propositions, contradiction, implication, qualifiers, and hidden modal claims. | Claim Stack and contradiction-pair repair on a paragraph from public argument. | Confused listener asks whether two statements can both be true. |

## Debate, Persuasion, And Disingenuous-Exchange Standard

CR-101 must train learners to persuade and change minds, not merely argue. A passing learner can:

- stop word games by locking definitions and identifying term drift;
- distinguish sincere confusion, honest disagreement, combative pressure, and public performance;
- name observable tactics without claiming hidden knowledge of motives;
- redirect a disingenuous exchange toward one claim, one premise, and one standard;
- adapt to different people without changing the truth claim;
- give the other person a dignified path to revise their own position;
- protect honest listeners when the direct opponent is performing for the room;
- leave an exchange when it is no longer reason-responsive.

## Change Research Standard

CR-101 treats change as a reasoning, persuasion, and coaching problem. A learner must be able to help another person see the need for change and take ownership of a next step without coercion, humiliation, or hidden pressure.

The course uses public-safe summaries of change research, including motivational interviewing, readiness-to-change, self-determination, self-efficacy, implementation intentions, habit and environment design, cognitive dissonance, attitude-change and inoculation, reactance, social norms and diffusion, adult learning, feedback, and deliberate practice.

A passing learner can:

- define the target change as concrete behavior rather than vague character judgment;
- identify readiness before giving advice;
- reflect ambivalence and values in the other person's own terms;
- preserve autonomy, competence, and relationship while persuading;
- turn insight into an if-then plan with cue, obstacle, support, and follow-up;
- anticipate relapse or counter-pressure without shame;
- use social proof, trusted messengers, and norms ethically;
- distinguish helping a person change from controlling a person.

## Practice

Practice includes distinction ladders, definition trees, proof reconstruction, syllogism tests, question ladders, fallacy repair, ethical speech outlines, steelman drills, teach-back, capstone defense, the Debate And Persuasion Practice Lab, and the Change Facilitation Research Lab. Live practice must be ordinary, consent-respecting, low-stakes, and never hidden testing.

## Quizzes

Quizzes support retrieval of vocabulary, argument forms, fallacy patterns, and source-move names. They are calibration tools only; mastery requires essays, spoken explanations, dialogue performance, revision, and a defended portfolio.

## Tests

Major tests:

- Baseline essay: diagnose a confused argument without humiliating the speaker.
- Midterm fallacy repair packet: identify the error, steelman the best version, and rebuild the argument.
- Spoken explanation: explain a proof-like argument without reading notes.
- Capstone defense: present a portfolio, answer objections, and revise.

## Projects

Projects:

- Definition discipline packet.
- Proof reconstruction.
- Written dialogue with ordered questions.
- Ethical speech outline.
- Change facilitation plan.
- Modern claim audit.
- Clear reasoning capstone portfolio.

## Portfolio Evidence

The portfolio must show notes, corrected drills, written analysis, spoken explanation transcript or recording reference, dialogue work, capstone artifacts, feedback, and revision. Passive watching is not mastery; passive watching cannot substitute for practice evidence.

## Accessibility Notes

Lectures need captions, transcript, board close-ups for argument maps, text alternatives for diagrams, written alternatives for spoken practice when needed, and non-timed assessment options unless timing is part of a specific performance objective.

## Adaptive Remediation

If a learner struggles, the teacher should route to the missed objective, use a simpler claim, ask for retrieval, apply the method to a new argument, and collect a revised artifact. Remediation should change examples and pressure level without lowering the reasoning standard.
