# Clear Reasoning Misconceptions

- `winning-equals-reasoning` - Learner treats rhetorical victory as proof of clear thought.
- `definition-after-argument` - Learner argues before stabilizing key terms.
- `fallacy-label-as-answer` - Learner labels a fallacy without explaining the structure or repair.
- `charity-is-weakness` - Learner thinks representing an opponent fairly means agreeing with them.
- `proof-is-only-math` - Learner fails to transfer ordered proof craft to everyday arguments.
- `persuasion-is-manipulation` - Learner ignores the difference between ethical adaptation and hidden pressure.
- `confidence-equals-clarity` - Learner mistakes speed and fluency for disciplined reasoning.
- `information-causes-change` - Learner assumes that explaining the facts is enough to change behavior.
- `pressure-causes-ownership` - Learner mistakes compliance under pressure for durable self-owned change.
- `readiness-does-not-matter` - Learner gives advice before identifying awareness, ambivalence, preparation, action, maintenance, or relapse state.
- `relapse-proves-failure` - Learner treats a setback as proof that change was fake instead of data for repair.

## Remediation Guidance

When a misconception appears, the teacher should ask the learner to slow down, define terms, map the argument, steelman the best opposing version, identify readiness for change, preserve autonomy, and rewrite the claim or plan with evidence, limits, next action, and relapse repair.
