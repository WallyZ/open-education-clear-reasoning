## Pedagogical Use

- Reasoning move trained: Classifying terms and identifying category mistakes.
- Module or track: Terms, Definitions, And Categories; Grammar And Meaning.
- Practice drill: Term Lock and Definition Tree.
- Assessment artifact: Repair a confused argument by stabilizing its key terms and categories.
- Transfer case: Analyze a public argument where a person, action, institution, value, and quantity are being mixed.

## Cultural Context

- Knowledge standard: Terms and predicates must be stabilized before inference can be tested.
- Argument goal: Clarify what is being said and what kind of claim it is.
- Disagreement norm: Dispute the classification or predication before disputing downstream conclusions.
- Authority structure: Classical text, teacher-guided classification, examples, and counterexamples.
- Cultural caveat: Aristotelian categories are not a neutral universal taxonomy for every civilization; they are a Western logical tool that must be taught as such.
- Misread risks: Treating categories as labels to win arguments; ignoring borderline cases; forcing non-Western categories into Greek terms without caveat.
