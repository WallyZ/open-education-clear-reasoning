# Course Delivery, Practice, And Progress Guide

Clear reasoning instruction should adapt to the learner while preserving disciplined argument, charity, performance, and revision standards.

## Delivery Rules

- Teach one learner at a time from their current evidence.
- Cover the material even when examples, pressure level, and practice format change.
- Use short retrieval prompts during lectures.
- Encourage handwritten notes, argument maps, rewritten definitions, and revision after critique.
- Keep live disagreement practice consent-respecting, low-stakes, and ordinary.
- No hidden testing, humiliation, covert recording, or pressure to disclose private beliefs.

## Progress Evidence

Progress requires artifacts:

- distinction ladders
- definition trees
- proposition maps
- proof reconstructions
- fallacy repair packets
- written dialogues
- spoken explanation records
- revised capstone portfolio

## Adaptation Loop

1. Diagnose the objective and misconception.
2. Reduce the claim or argument complexity.
3. Ask for retrieval before giving another example.
4. Apply the method to a new argument.
5. Collect a revised artifact.
6. Decide whether to advance, remediate, or assign spaced review.
