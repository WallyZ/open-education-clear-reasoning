# Curriculum Matrix

## Purpose

The curriculum matrix ties each course area to a Western anchor, comparative lens, cultural caveat, practice, and assessment. It prevents the program from becoming either a Western-only survey or a shallow comparative sampler.

## Matrix Rules

Every course unit must include:

- primary Western anchor
- optional comparative lens
- skill trained
- cultural caveat
- practice drill
- assessment artifact
- reviewer status

## Foundation Matrix

| Unit Area | Western Anchor | Comparative Lens | Cultural Caveat | Practice | Assessment |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Attention and distinction | Plato, Aristotle, Stoics | Confucian restraint and role-aware speech | Confucian speech discipline aims at relational order, not merely individual debate skill. | Distinction ladder and speech restraint reflection. | Explain a confused debate and name what should be said, delayed, or left unsaid. |
| Terms and categories | Aristotle, Porphyry, Boethius | Nyaya categories as later comparative extension | Category systems are not interchangeable taxonomies; each serves a larger metaphysical and pedagogical aim. | Definition tree and borderline-case test. | Repair a term-shift argument and explain the classification choice. |
| Propositions and contradiction | Aristotle, Port-Royal Logic | Buddhist debate/refutation as later comparative extension | Some traditions use contradiction differently when diagnosing illusion, attachment, or mistaken views. | Proposition extraction and contradiction pairs. | Map claims and name whether conflict is logical, verbal, or contextual. |
| Proof and demonstration | Euclid, Aristotle, Newton | Islamic falsafa and mathematical commentary as later extension | Demonstration can sit inside commentary, theology, or mathematical practice rather than modern classroom proof alone. | Euclidean step test. | Reconstruct a proof-like argument and defend every step. |
| Syllogism and inference | Aristotle, medieval logic | Nyaya five-member inference as later extension | Inference forms carry assumptions about audience, example, and warrant. | Syllogism translation. | Compare argument form and explain what each form makes visible. |
| Demonstration and causes | Aristotle, Aquinas, Bacon | Islamic kalam/falsafa and Buddhist causation as later extension | Cause, purpose, and explanation are culturally loaded; do not force one causal vocabulary onto all traditions. | Cause ladder. | Explain a claim by separating that, why, cause, and condition. |
| Dialectic and disputation | Plato, Aristotle, Abelard, Aquinas | Jewish legal reasoning and Buddhist debate as later extension | Some disagreement traditions preserve multiple live positions rather than forcing closure. | Objection box and concession map. | Conduct structured disputation with objections and replies. |
| Fallacies and sophistry | Aristotle, Cicero, diagnostic eristic works | Mohist standards and strategy traditions as later extension | Bad-faith tactics vary by culture and setting; do not treat all indirect speech as manipulation. | Eristic shield. | Repair a combative exchange without mirroring abuse. |
| Rhetoric and speech | Aristotle, Isocrates, Cicero, Quintilian, Augustine | African oral counsel and Chinese rhetoric as later extension | Oral, proverb, and role-based speech may reason differently from written debate. | Speech compression and audience adaptation. | Deliver a speech and justify arrangement for the audience. |
| Modern transfer | Bacon, Hume, Mill, Peirce, Federalist Papers, Tocqueville | Comparative civic judgment traditions | Institutions, legitimacy, and public reason differ across cultures; name the governing assumptions. | Evidence and incentive map. | Analyze a modern claim with uncertainty and institutional context. |

## Matrix Completion Standard

A row is lesson-ready only when:

- the Western anchor has a source packet or remains reference-only with no excerpts
- comparative lane is marked reviewed or deferred
- cultural caveat is explicit
- practice is concrete
- assessment requires transfer
- reviewer status is recorded
