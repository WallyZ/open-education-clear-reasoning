# Canon Integration Map

## Purpose

This program is not a history survey. Each source is used because it trains a reusable reasoning move: define, distinguish, infer, demonstrate, inquire, object, reply, persuade, judge, or teach.

The integration map prevents the canon from becoming decorative. A source is useful only when it becomes a practice, an assessment, and a transferable habit.

For comparative traditions, this map must be used together with the civilization coverage and culture-difference framework. The Western spine remains primary; comparative lanes add contrast and enrichment only after source and cultural review gates are satisfied.

## Integration Stages

| Stage | Meaning | Required Evidence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `reference_only` | Work may be cited as an influence, but no source text is copied here. | Source listed with use boundary. |
| `packet_ready` | A source packet documents edition, license/public-domain evidence, URL or library reference, attribution, and excerpt boundary. | Source packet file and verification evidence. |
| `lesson_ready` | Original lesson and exercises are written against the source move. | Lesson, drills, assessment, and rubric. |
| `performance_ready` | Learner must use the move under spoken, written, or adversarial pressure. | Performance prompt, rubric, and revision path. |

Until a packet is ready, every source is treated as reference-only.

## Integration Lanes

| Lane | Source Families | Trained Move | Practice Form | Assessment Form |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Grammar and meaning | Aristotle, Porphyry, Boethius, Locke, Port-Royal Logic | Stabilize terms and classify meanings. | Definition tree, predication map, ambiguity repair. | Repair an argument whose key term shifts. |
| Demonstration and proof | Euclid, Archimedes, Aristotle, Newton | Build visible stepwise proof. | Proof labeling, missing-step insertion, construction explanation. | Reconstruct an argument from definitions and accepted premises. |
| Dialectic and disputation | Plato, Aristotle, Abelard, Aquinas | Ask ordered questions and answer strong objections. | Question ladder, objection box, concession map. | Conduct a structured disputation with objections and replies. |
| Rhetoric and public speech | Isocrates, Aristotle, Cicero, Quintilian, Augustine, Lincoln, Douglass | Persuade ethically with audience awareness. | Arrangement outline, ethos/pathos/logos audit, speech compression. | Deliver a short persuasive speech with source-grounded reasoning. |
| Inquiry and evidence | Bacon, Galileo, Mill, Peirce, Darwin | Separate observation, cause, induction, probability, and explanation. | Induction ladder, causal rival map, belief-fixation audit. | Analyze an evidence claim and state what would change the conclusion. |
| Civic and moral judgment | Aristotle, Cicero, Confucius, Epictetus, Smith, Federalist Papers, Tocqueville | Reason about duty, character, incentives, institutions, liberty, and consequence. | Duty-conflict map, institutional incentive map, prudence case. | Defend a civic judgment while naming tradeoffs and limiting claims. |
| Strategy and adversarial reasoning | Sun Tzu, Machiavelli, Clausewitz, Sophistical Refutations, diagnostic eristic references | Recognize adversarial moves and preserve discipline. | Eristic shield, bad-faith reset, frame-control audit. | Stay accurate under combative questioning without mirroring manipulation. |
| Teacher formation | Quintilian, Augustine, Aquinas, selected modern pedagogy references | Teach reasoning moves and coach correction. | Teach-back, error diagnosis, revision conference. | Teach a skill to another learner and document the correction path. |

## Reference-Only Modern Sources

Some useful modern works are not public-domain and must remain reference-only unless explicit rights are obtained. They can inform original lesson design, but this repo must not copy their text, problem sets, diagrams, or proprietary terminology-heavy frameworks.

Potential reference-only categories:

- argument model and informal logic traditions
- cognitive bias and judgment research
- negotiation and conflict-resolution research
- visual explanation and data communication
- modern rhetoric, debate, and composition pedagogy

## Integration Rule

A great work is integrated only when the program can answer all seven questions:

1. What reasoning move does this source train?
2. What common modern confusion does the move solve?
3. What daily drill builds the habit?
4. What written artifact proves understanding?
5. What spoken performance proves transfer?
6. What misuse or distortion must be guarded against?
7. What revision path moves a weak attempt toward mastery?
