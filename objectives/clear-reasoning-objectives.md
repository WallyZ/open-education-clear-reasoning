# Clear Reasoning Objectives

## CR-101

- `clear-reasoning:objectives/course/cr-101/attention-and-distinction`: Slow down, identify the kind of question being asked, and separate related claims.
- `clear-reasoning:objectives/course/cr-101/definitions-and-categories`: Define terms, test borderline cases, and repair category mistakes.
- `clear-reasoning:objectives/course/cr-101/propositions-and-contradiction`: Extract propositions and identify contradiction, implication, and qualification.
- `clear-reasoning:objectives/course/cr-101/proof-craft`: Reconstruct proof-like reasoning from definitions, premises, and ordered steps.
- `clear-reasoning:objectives/course/cr-101/inference-and-validity`: Distinguish valid form, strong support, weak support, and persuasive appearance.
- `clear-reasoning:objectives/course/cr-101/dialectical-questions`: Ask ordered questions that reveal structure without merely attacking.
- `clear-reasoning:objectives/course/cr-101/fallacy-repair`: Detect fallacies and manipulative moves, then repair the argument into a fairer form.
- `clear-reasoning:objectives/course/cr-101/ethical-rhetoric`: Persuade without hiding weak reasoning or manipulating the audience.
- `clear-reasoning:objectives/course/cr-101/disagreement-discipline`: Steelman and answer an opposing view with discipline and charity.
- `clear-reasoning:objectives/course/cr-101/capstone-defense`: Defend and revise a clear reasoning portfolio in writing, speech, and dialogue.
